Getting Started

Choose Your Role

On first launch, Terminus asks how you want to use it. Your choice shapes the entire experience — both modes share the same 3D trail view, but display different information and serve different purposes.

I'm Running

You are on the trail. The app tracks your GPS position, shows live navigation and route analytics, and broadcasts your location to anyone following you.

I'm Following

You are watching a runner from anywhere. Enter their room code to see their live position moving along the 3D trail in real time.

Runner Setup

Pick a Race

After choosing the runner role, select the race or trail you are participating in. Terminus loads and processes the full GPX route — parsing elevation, detecting climbs, identifying peaks, and computing section analytics.

Once loaded, you land directly in the 3D trail view with your position tracked live.

Your room code is generated automatically. Share it with supporters before the race so they can follow your progress as soon as you start moving.

Visualization

The Trail View

The main screen renders your trail in full 3D with real elevation. Pan, rotate, and zoom freely to explore the route ahead.

Action How to do it
Rotate Drag (one finger / left-click drag)
Pan Two-finger drag / right-click drag
Zoom Pinch / scroll wheel

2D Profile Mode

Flatten the view into an elevation profile to read the shape of climbs and descents at a glance. Toggle back to 3D anytime.

Slope Colors

Color the trail by gradient — light for flat, dark for steep — so you can anticipate hard sections before you reach them.

Peak Labels

Named summits appear as labels directly on the trail. They fade based on camera distance to stay readable without clutter.

Off-Course Alert

Stray from the route and the scene flashes with how far off you are. Clears automatically once you're back on track.

Navigation

Top Panel

The top panel is your real-time navigation feed. It shows what's immediately ahead on the route, updating automatically as you progress.

Each card represents the next waypoint on your route and shows the direction to take (arrow icon), the distance remaining to reach it, and the elevation gain and loss for that leg. Cards animate out as waypoints are completed and new ones slide in — always keeping the next section front and center.

In Follower mode, the top panel switches to a location freshness indicator — showing how long ago the runner's last position update was received, color-coded from green (fresh) to red (stale, 15min+).

Analytics

Bottom Panel

Swipe up from the bottom to expand the panel. Even when closed, the bar always shows three key numbers: distance remaining, ETA, and time remaining.

Scroll through the expanded panel to access all trail analytics.

Trail Overview

Trail name, total distance, total elevation gain/loss, max completion time, slowest required pace, and a start/elapsed time counter.

Elevation Profile

A visual chart of the full route's elevation — useful for planning pacing strategy and spotting major climbs at a glance.

Trail Progression

A progress bar showing how far along the trail you are, plus cumulative elevation gain and loss accumulated so far.

Stage & Section Analytics

Per-stage and per-leg breakdowns with distance, elevation gain/loss, difficulty rating, cut-off times, and required pace targets.

Stage ETA

Estimated arrival times for upcoming stages based on your current pace and the difficulty of the terrain ahead.

Climb Pro

Every significant ascent automatically detected from the elevation data. For each climb: distance to start (or "Completed"), summit elevation, climb length, total gain, and average gradient. The current climb is highlighted.

Peak Summary

A list of all named peaks and high points detected along the route.

Actions

Trail management controls: Fly-by mode toggle, flush saved locations, and leave trail. See the Commands section below.

Follower Mode

Watching a Runner

As a follower, enter the runner's room code on the start screen. You land in the same 3D trail view — but instead of your own position, you see the runner's live location moving along the trail.

All stats — distance, ETA, climbs, progression — reflect the runner's position, not yours. Multiple followers can watch the same runner simultaneously.

Location Freshness (Top Panel)

Instead of navigation cards, the top panel shows how long ago the last position update was received — color-coded from green (fresh, seconds ago) through yellow (a few minutes) to red (stale, 15min+).

Multi-Follower Sessions

The same room code works for any number of followers. Everyone sees the same live position simultaneously.

No Broadcasting

Followers don't broadcast a position — the Share button is not available in follower mode. All other scene controls (fly-by, slope, profile, theme) work identically.

Progressive Web App

Install & Notifications

Terminus is a fully installable Progressive Web App. Once installed it runs as a standalone app — no browser chrome — and works offline after the first load.

iOS & Android

Open Terminus in Safari (iOS) or Chrome (Android), tap the Share button, then choose Add to Home Screen. The app icon appears like any native app and launches full-screen.

Desktop

In Chrome or Edge, look for the Install icon in the address bar (or open the browser menu and choose Install Terminus). The app opens in its own window, separate from your browser.

Works Offline

All app assets are precached on first load. The 3D trail view, analytics, and route data are available without an internet connection. Only live GPS sharing requires connectivity.

Auto-Updates

When a new version is available, it downloads silently in the background. The update is applied the next time you open the app — no manual action needed.

Push Notifications (Follower)

As a follower, grant notification permission when prompted. You will receive a notification each time the runner's position updates — even when the app is backgrounded or fully closed. Tap the notification to instantly open or focus the app.

Notifications require the app to be installed as a PWA on iOS (Safari) and are supported natively on Android and desktop. If you accidentally deny permission, re-enable it in your device or browser settings under Terminus → Notifications.
